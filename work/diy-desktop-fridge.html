DIY Desktop Fridge

Learn how to build a budget-friendly desktop fridge project for just $15. Using a 12V DC power supply and a thermoelectric Peltier module, you can create a cooling system capable of chilling items up to 25°C. This DIY project is ideal for school exhibitions, providing a simple and cost-effective solution for your cooling needs.

Materials required

  • 12706 Peltier Module
  • 12V CPU Fan (x2)
  • 12V LED Strip
  • 12V 10Amp Power Supply
  • Heat Sink (x2)
  • Thermocol ice box
  • Thermal paste

Instructions

  1. Begin by securely fixing the heat sink onto the backside of the thermacol ice box.
  2. Attach a 12V CPU fan inside the box using hot glue, ensuring it is firmly in place.
  3. Install a vibrant 12V LED light strip on the interior of the box to provide illumination.
  4. Create neat holes on the backside of the box for the wires to pass through, and seal them effectively using hot glue.
  5. Apply a thin layer of thermal paste and carefully affix the peltier module onto the heat sink surface.
  6. Apply a small amount of thermal paste and securely mount the CPU fan (along with the heat sink) using hot glue.
  7. Refer to the circuit diagram provided and diligently connect all the wires, considering soldering if necessary.
  8. Use sticky tape to attach one side of the lid, creating a convenient door-like opening for easy access.
  9. Make a precise hole on the door and position magnets, firmly securing them in place with hot glue.
  10. Congratulations! Your impressive DIY Desktop Fridge, built using a thermacol ice box, is now ready for you to enjoy its cooling capabilities.
